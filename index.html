Our Origin

Founded by someone who had lived it.

Christopher O'Reilly grew up in The Liberties. After his own recovery from addiction, he went back to the same streets - this time with soup, sandwiches, and a table on Meath Street. No grant. No committee. Just one person who understood what it meant to need someone to show up.

What started as a single table has grown into a charity with two vans, 33 volunteers, a fixed weekly setup on Thomas Street, and a Ballyfermot run every Thursday and Friday - all donor-funded, all volunteer-run, not one night missed.

"It's just about giving people a chance to get their lives back."

- Christopher O'Reilly, Founder

The route that never stops.

The same streets, the same faces, the same commitment - five nights a week for the van, every Thursday for the full Thomas Street setup with tables and walking teams.

City-wide

What We Provide

More than a hot meal.

Every van and walking round carries more than soup - because a night on the street takes more than food to survive.

Hot Food & Drinks

Hot soup, sandwiches, tea and coffee - prepared fresh and served every night the run goes out.

Clothing & Toiletries

Donated clothing, hygiene products and basic essentials - sorted and distributed where needed most.

Sleeping Bags & Tents

Warmth and shelter for people sleeping rough - handed out directly, no conditions, no questions.

Referrals to Treatment

Not just feeding people - connecting them to residential treatment centres and helping families take the next steps toward stability.

Our Next Chapter

The café is coming.
Help us get there.

A nightly run keeps people going. A café gives them somewhere to come back to - a warm room, a hot meal sitting down, a door that's open in daylight too. After losing people on the streets in recent years, we're clear: we need more - more residential treatment, more drop-in centres. The café is where that chapter starts.

The Bigger Goal

The café is the first step. We need more access to residential treatment and more drop-in spaces - places people can walk into during the day, not just find at night.

Questions About the Campaign

Will the nightly run continue once the café opens?

+

Yes - the café is an addition to the nightly outreach, not a replacement. The van and walking teams keep running five nights a week regardless.

How will my donation be used?

+

Every donation funds frontline outreach directly - food, clothing, sleeping bags, van running costs. The organisation receives no government funding.

When will the café open?

+

No fixed date yet - the timeline depends on funding and finding the right space. Get on the supporter list to be the first to know.
